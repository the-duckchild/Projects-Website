Žagarė Fringe Festival, Lithuania

I delivered a workshop explaining how to build a rudimentary synthesier with basic electronic components to a mixed age group of local particpants. Žagarė Fringe Festival was established in 2015 as a biennial Fringe Festival that takes place in the northern Lithuanian town of Žagarė.

Absurd Instruments Hackathon

I was selected to take part in this weekend long Hackathon at Queen Mary's University, London. Participants were asked to take part in " Making absurd, surreal and silly musical propositions. Composing unconventional artefacts challenging current music technology. Subverting the ways we use musical tools to reveal unstated assumptions and explore alternatives."

Custom MIDI Controller

I designed, prototyped, programmed and built this Teensy driven midi controller for use in my live performances. In total ten faders, forty-three buttons and twenty-two potentiometers give control of the software Ableton Live for live "dub mixing" of audio tracks. The code can be found here

MB6582 SID Synth

I took on the challenge of building an MB6582 SID synth. The MB6582 is a fully featured synthesizer, built on the MIDIbox platform and leveraging the power of eight SID chips - originally used in the Commodore 64. The project is open source and the documentation can be found here.

DIY Instrument Making & Hacking - Onassis Stegi, Athens

I was selected to take part in and perform at this Hackathon event at Onassis Stegi Cultural centre in Athens, Greece. The event invited artists, musicians, sound engineers, designers and programmers in a three-day hackathon to collaboratively create new instruments and sounds, and explore new ways to make music, and was part of the part of the Interfaces project co-funded by the Creative Europe program of the European Union
